# Nepal Republic — Overview

**Nepal Republic** is an AI-powered civic intelligence platform that tracks the Nepal government's public commitments in real time, scores accountability, and empowers citizens to report issues, expose corruption, and verify evidence.

> Report. Track. Verify. AI-powered Nepal's civic platform.

This document is a high-level walkthrough of what the app is, why it exists, how it works, and where it's headed.

---

## The Problem

Nepal is a young democracy. Politicians make promises during campaigns, win elections, and then... silence. There is no systematic, neutral, evidence-based way for citizens to track whether the government is actually delivering on what it pledged.

Journalists cover individual stories. Civil society writes reports that nobody reads. Social media is noisy but fragmented. No single place pulls it all together and says: here is what was promised, here is what moved, here is what stalled, and here is what changed today.

That gap is what Nepal Republic fills.

---

## Why Now

A new generation of Nepali leaders — the RSP party, Balen Shah's mayoral tenure — has energized citizens who actually want to hold their government accountable. There is real demand for transparency, especially among young, urban, tech-savvy Nepalis and the massive diaspora community abroad.

This is not a partisan project. Nepal Republic tracks ALL government commitments regardless of which party made them. The goal is accountability, not advocacy for any side.

## What It Does

At its core, Nepal Republic is five things:

1. **A commitment tracker** — 109 government commitments, each with a status (not started / in progress / delivered / stalled), progress percentage, letter grade (A–F), responsible officials, and a full evidence trail.

2. **An AI-powered intelligence engine** — It automatically scans 80+ sources across the entire Nepali media landscape, classifies what it finds, and connects signals to commitments. No human editors picking stories. The AI reads everything.

3. **A civic issues platform** — Citizens can report real problems (infrastructure failures, service gaps, safety concerns), track resolution, and amplify each other's complaints.

4. **A corruption tracker** — Documented corruption cases with evidence trails, entity profiles, severity ratings, and AI-discovered patterns.

5. **A public scorecard** — A single grade for the government and for each ministry, updated in real time as evidence comes in.

## The Intelligence Engine

This is the brain of the platform and where the real differentiation lives.

### How It Works

The engine continuously scans 80+ sources across the Nepali media landscape, classifies every signal using AI, and connects relevant findings to tracked commitments. It runs autonomously — no human editors required for intake.

### What It Scans

The AI monitors sources across multiple categories:

- **News media** — Major English and Nepali-language news outlets including Kathmandu Post, Republica, Himalayan Times, Online Khabar, Nepali Times, Gorkhapatra, Ratopati, and more
- **Government portals** — Ministry websites, press releases, official notices, budget documents
- **Parliamentary records** — Bills, acts, Nepal Gazette, committee proceedings
- **Video and audio** — YouTube news channels, press conferences, and political speeches — automatically transcribed to searchable text
- **Social media** — Political conversations across Facebook, Reddit, X/Twitter, TikTok, Threads, and Telegram
- **International organizations** — Reports and publications from think tanks, development partners, and policy organizations
- **Trending data** — Real-time and daily trending searches related to Nepal governance

### What the AI Does With It

1. **Classifies** — Every incoming signal is analyzed: does it confirm progress, contradict a claim, announce new policy, or report budget activity?
2. **Matches** — Relevant signals are connected to specific commitments using keyword matching and AI context
3. **Scores** — Progress percentages and letter grades are updated based on the weight of evidence
4. **Briefs** — Plain-language summaries are generated for each commitment, explaining what's happening in simple terms
5. **Discovers** — The AI detects when politicians announce NEW commitments not yet being tracked and flags them for review
6. **Deduplicates** — The same news story from 10 outlets does not count as 10 signals. Smart matching prevents inflation.
7. **Flags conflicts** — When two sources contradict each other, the AI flags it for review rather than picking a side
8. **Translates** — Nepali-language signals are auto-translated for cross-referencing with English sources

### How Scoring Works

Every commitment is scored using verified evidence from multiple independent sources:

- Official government updates and announcements
- Independent news coverage and investigative reports
- Budget allocation and actual expenditure data
- Citizen-submitted evidence (photos, documents, links)
- Source reliability and cross-reference consistency

The AI cross-references government claims against independent reporting before updating any score. When sources conflict, the platform flags it as disputed rather than defaulting to the government's version.

Grades can go down. If a commitment stalls, funding is cut, or contradictory evidence emerges, the progress and grade decrease. The system reflects reality, not just announcements.

## What Makes It Different

**AI-first, not manual.** There are no human editors deciding which stories matter. The engine reads everything across every platform and connects the dots automatically. Humans review edge cases, not routine classification.

**Cross-platform intelligence.** Most accountability tools rely on one or two sources. Nepal Republic sees across news, YouTube, Facebook, Reddit, TikTok, X, Telegram, government portals, and Parliament simultaneously. No single-platform bias.

**More than a tracker.** This isn't just a spreadsheet of promises. Citizens can report civic issues, track corruption, submit evidence, vote on claims, and participate in verification. It's a two-way civic platform.

**Evidence-based.** Every status change is backed by source links. No opinions, no editorial spin. Citizens can click through to the original evidence and judge for themselves.

**Bilingual from day one.** Full Nepali language support is built into the architecture, not bolted on after launch. Every feature, every label, every AI-generated briefing works in both languages.

**Designed for replication.** The platform architecture is country-agnostic. Swap the commitments, swap the news sources, and the same engine works for any democracy.

**Open for participation.** Citizens can submit feedback, propose new tracking items, contribute evidence, report corruption, file civic complaints, and participate in verification.

## The Vision

Start with Nepal. Prove the model works. Then expand.

Every democracy deserves a public scorecard — a neutral, AI-powered system that tracks what the government promised versus what it delivered. ## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is Nepal Republic?
Nepal Republic is an AI-powered civic intelligence platform. It tracks 109 government commitments, scores accountability with letter grades, and lets citizens report issues, expose corruption, and verify evidence — all powered by AI scanning 80+ sources daily.

### Who runs it?
Nepal Republic is an independent civic technology initiative. It is not affiliated with any political party, government body, or media organization.

### Is it free?
Yes. Completely free and open to all citizens.

### Do I need an account?
No. You can browse everything without an account. Creating an account unlocks features like submitting evidence, voting, reporting issues, and building a watchlist.

### Where does the data come from?
The AI scans 80+ sources daily — major Nepali news outlets, government portals, parliamentary records, social media, budget documents, and international reports. Every claim on the platform links back to its source.

### How accurate is it?
Every claim is linked to its source. The AI cross-references multiple sources before updating status. When sources conflict, the platform flags it as disputed rather than picking a side. Citizens can also submit evidence to correct or verify claims.

### Can the AI make mistakes?
Yes. That's why every AI-generated insight links to its source, and the platform has community verification. The system is transparent about its confidence level.

### Is it biased?
Nepal Republic tracks ALL government commitments regardless of which party made them. The AI follows evidence, not editorial lines. The goal is accountability, not advocacy.

### How is the government scored?
The overall score combines five factors: delivery rate, average progress, trust score (verification quality), budget utilization, and citizen sentiment (public voting). Each factor is weighted and combined into a 0–100 score with an A–F letter grade.

### Can I contribute?
Yes — submit evidence (photos, documents, links), vote on commitments, report civic issues, report corruption, and share findings. You can also apply to become a verified contributor with enhanced review capabilities.

### Can I report things anonymously?
Yes. Corruption reports and civic complaints can be submitted without identifying yourself.

### Does it work on mobile?
Yes. Fully responsive and available as a Progressive Web App — install it on your phone's home screen for an app-like experience.

### What languages are supported?
English and Nepali (नेपाली). Switch between them at any time using the language toggle.

### Can this work for other countries?
The platform is designed to be country-agnostic. The architecture can be adapted for any democracy by changing the commitments and sources.
